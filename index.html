Context & Background

Agriculture depends on local conditions such as soil, climate, seasonality, and water availability. Farmers must continuously make informed decisions about crop selection, sowing, irrigation, fertilization, pest management, and harvesting. However, access to reliable, localized agricultural expertise remains limited.

This challenge emphasizes agentic reasoning and domain knowledge engineering, not just conversational AI.

Pest & Disease Reference

Comprehensive pest and disease management is crucial for successful crop production. Below are key resources and reference materials to help structure your knowledge base.

üêõ Common Agricultural Pests

Insect Pests:

  • Aphids - Sap-sucking insects affecting multiple crops
  • Whiteflies - Virus vectors in vegetables and cotton
  • Stem Borers - Major threat to cereals and sugarcane
  • Fruit Flies - Damage to fruits and vegetables
  • Leaf Miners - Tunneling damage in leaves

Other Pests:

  • Rodents and birds
  • Nematodes
  • Mites

ü¶† Common Crop Diseases

Fungal Diseases:

  • Powdery Mildew - White powder on leaves
  • Rust - Orange/brown pustules on leaves
  • Blight - Rapid leaf death
  • Root Rot - Fungal damage to roots

Bacterial & Viral:

  • Bacterial wilt
  • Mosaic viruses
  • Leaf spot diseases

üîç Identification Methods

  • Visual Symptoms: Leaf discoloration, wilting, spots
  • Growth Patterns: Stunted growth, abnormal development
  • Damage Signs: Holes, chewed edges, tunnels
  • Timing: Seasonal occurrence patterns
  • Location: Field position and crop part affected

üõ°Ô∏è Management Strategies

Integrated Pest Management (IPM):

  • Cultural Control: Crop rotation, resistant varieties
  • Biological Control: Natural predators, beneficial insects
  • Mechanical Control: Traps, barriers, hand-picking
  • Chemical Control: Selective pesticides (last resort)
  • Prevention: Sanitation, proper spacing, monitoring
